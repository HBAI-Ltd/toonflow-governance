# Toonflow Community Governance Charter

**v1.0 · March 24, 2026 · HBAI-Ltd**

---

## I. General Provisions

This Charter defines the composition, rights, and obligations of the Toonflow Community Council, as well as the community fund revenue sharing mechanism.

---

## II. Community Council

### 2.1 Composition

The Community Council consists of **15 seats** in total:

| Seat Type | Quantity | Election/Appointment Method |
|---------|------|---------|
| Community Contributor Seats | 11 Seats | Elected by all valid contributors |
| HBAI Appointed Seats | 4 Seats | Appointed internally by HBAI-Ltd |

### 2.2 Election Rules for Contributor Seats

**Eligibility to Run:**
- Must rank within the top 30 in annual contribution points to be eligible to run.
- Historical cumulative points must be ≥ 200 (to prevent short-term point farming).

**Voting Rules:**
- Voting Rights: All contributors with annual points ≥ 50 are eligible to vote.
- Each voter can cast up to 11 votes; the top 11 candidates with the most votes are elected.
- Election results will be publicly announced, including the vote count for each candidate.

**Term Limits:**
- The term is 1 year. Members may seek re-election upon term expiration.
- The same individual may serve no more than **2 consecutive terms** (to prevent seat entrenchment).

**Absence & Vacancy Policy:**
- Two consecutive unexcused absences from Council meetings will result in automatic replacement by the candidate who placed 12th in the annual points ranking.
- Voluntary resignation requires 30 days' written notice, after which the seat will be filled by the next eligible candidate in the points ranking.

### 2.3 Rules for HBAI Appointed Seats

- Appointed internally by HBAI-Ltd to represent the company's strategic direction.
- The list of appointees is publicly announced annually.
- The 4 HBAI seats hold **ultimate veto power** (reserved for protecting the core direction of the project and the fundamental interests of the community).
- Any exercise of veto power must include a stated reason documented in the public meeting minutes.

## III. Rights and Obligations of the Council

### 3.1 Rights

| Right | Description |
|------|------|
| **Community Fund Priority Voting Right** | Vote quarterly on the allocation priorities of the community fund; executed upon majority approval. |
| **Annual Revenue Sharing Proposal Right** | The Council may propose annual revenue distribution plans, subject to final confirmation by HBAI. |
| **Roadmap Right to Know** | Advance knowledge of the Toonflow product roadmap, with the ability to participate in discussions prior to public release. |
| **Priority Closed Beta Access** | Priority access to beta testing for new versions and features. |
| **Official Representation** | Council members may participate in external events under the official title of "Toonflow Community Council Member". |

### 3.2 Obligations

| Obligation | Description |
|------|------|
| **Meeting Attendance** | Must attend quarterly Council meetings unless exceptional circumstances arise (online or offline). |
| **Disclosure Obligation** | Publicly release meeting summaries and voting results within 7 working days after each meeting. |
| **Neutrality Obligation** | Votes must prioritize the overall interests of the community. Self-dealing or voting on behalf of personal/external commercial interests is strictly prohibited. |
| **Confidentiality** | Undisclosed information, such as product roadmaps, must not be leaked prior to official release. |
| **Code of Conduct** | Must strictly abide by the Toonflow Community Code of Conduct. The public words and actions of Council members reflect the community's image. |

### 3.3 Regarding Revenue Sharing (Dividends)

- **Council seats inherently carry NO revenue sharing eligibility**—governance rights and economic rights are strictly separated.
- If Council members also hold personal contribution points, they participate in the community fund revenue sharing pool **strictly as regular developers**. Points are neither weighted nor discounted based on Council status.
- The above rules apply equally to members holding HBAI appointed seats.

## V. Community Fund Revenue Sharing Mechanism

### 5.1 Funding Source

**50%** of all commercial licensing revenue is injected into the Community Fund. Funds are allocated and publicly disclosed on a quarterly basis.

### 5.2 Revenue Sharing Eligibility

- **Participation Threshold:** Annual points ≥ 50.
- **Distribution Method:** Allocated proportionally based on an individual's annual points relative to the total sum of points held by all valid contributors.
- **Scope of Distribution:** All contributors meeting the threshold, including Council members (participating via personal points; seats carry no weighting).

### 5.3 Distribution Calculation Example

> Assuming in a given year:
> - Community Fund Revenue Sharing Pool: $200,000
> - Contributors meeting the threshold (≥50 points): 40 people
> - Total annual points of all valid contributors: 10,000 points
> - Annual points of Contributor A: 500 points
>
> **Contributor A's Share = $200,000 × (500 / 10,000) = $10,000**

### 5.4 Transparency Mechanism

| Frequency | Content |
|------|------|
| Quarterly | Publish total licensing revenue + amount allocated to the Community Fund. |
| Semi-Annually | Publish detailed usage reports of the Community Fund. |
| Annually | Publish the revenue sharing roster (including individual points and distributed amounts; individuals may opt for anonymity). |

## VI. Transitional Arrangements

Prior to the completion of the first official election (expected within 6 months):

- Licensing decision-making authority will temporarily be exercised by the Interim Founding Council (via appointment).
- HBAI-Ltd will temporarily act as the legal entity and assume associated legal responsibilities.
- Licensing agreements will be signed with HBAI-Ltd as the contracting entity.
- Community Observers will participate in all licensing decision discussions from start to finish.
- Upon completion of the election, licensing authority will be officially transferred to the Council, and the conditions for changing the legal entity will be determined by a Council vote.

## VII. Charter Amendments

- Amendments to this Charter require a passing vote by the Council (≥ 7 affirmative votes out of the 11 Contributor Seats) plus final confirmation by HBAI.
- Proposed amendments must be published as a draft 14 days in advance to allow for community feedback.
- Each amendment must record the version number and a changelog. Historical versions will be archived and kept publicly accessible.
